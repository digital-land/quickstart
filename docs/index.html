This page contains a series of notes and examples to help you get started building using Planning Data.

Data availabilty and quality

The Planning Data platform collects planning and housing data from a wide range of sources and transforms it into a consistent state across England.

Note the Planning Data Platform is in development. Where we have data, it may not yet cover the whole country—there may be things you expect to see that are currently missing.

Data licensing

The data is open, meaning anyone can view, build services using the data or download and analyse the data to inform investment and other decisions.

We believe making government planning and housing data easier to find, use and trust will lead to:

  • Better planning decisions
  • Fewer uncertainties and delays in the planning and housing systems
  • More new digital services for property and planning
  • Better relationships between communities and development

Contributing

We work in the open, and welcome your feedback and contributions. You can help:

  • identify missing data, and inform the design of the datasets we put on the platform by participating in the data design process
  • prioritise our work by commenting on our project backlogs
  • improve this guidance through by making a pull-request to the quickstart repository or participating in the quickstart GitHub discussion.
